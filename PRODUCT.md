# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Developers, QA engineers, SREs, support staff, and other people who frequently inspect logs, APIs, and event times in the browser. They need a fast, low-friction popup for checking the current Unix timestamp and converting between timestamps and human-readable dates without leaving their current workflow.

## Product Purpose

This browser extension provides immediate timestamp lookup and bidirectional time conversion in a compact popup. It exists to remove context switching during debugging, testing, and operational work. Success means users can open the extension, get the current timestamp in the needed precision, convert values in either direction, and copy results with minimal effort or cognitive load.

## Brand Personality

Professional, clear, immediate. The interface should feel dependable and efficient, with visual restraint that supports fast task completion rather than decoration.

## Anti-references

Do not make this feel like a flashy marketing landing page. Avoid over-decoration, novelty-first interactions, or visual effects that reduce readability. Do not chase a generic "modern" look at the expense of clarity, legibility, or speed.

## Design Principles

- Minimize time-to-answer for the primary timestamp tasks.
- Keep the UI compact, predictable, and easy to scan in a small browser popup.
- Prioritize readability over visual flourish.
- Preserve clear interaction feedback for copy, convert, and input states.
- Reduce user mistakes with straightforward labels, validation, and state transitions.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target at least WCAG AA. Preserve visible focus states, avoid relying on color alone to communicate state, and keep motion restrained so the interface remains comfortable for users who prefer reduced motion.
